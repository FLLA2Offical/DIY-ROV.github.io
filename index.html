DIY Underwater ROV

This is a low-cost remote operated vehicle designed and built to introduce youth to underwater exploration. The goal is to build a relatively low-cost underwater ROV that kids could build and use in their local water ways. We chose an ROV because of the popularity of underwater robotics and the many engineering challenges it presents, such as waterproofing and underwater maneuvering.

Challenges

Due to budget restrictions and the difficulty in obtaining parts, we faced significant challenges. We opted to use the Blue Robotics T100 thrusters for reliable underwater propulsion. Waterproofing and control systems posed major hurdles.

Cost

The total project cost is kept low, using budget-friendly materials and salvaged components where possible.

Goal

Our main goal is to design a functional underwater ROV accessible to youth groups, fostering STEM learning and practical engineering skills.

Materials

  • Blue Robotics T100 Thrusters
  • Arduino Microcontroller
  • Waterproof Housing
  • Various Electronic Components

Design

The ROV design incorporates modular thruster placement, waterproof compartments, and easy assembly for education.

Power

Power is supplied through a tethered battery pack on the surface, connected via waterproof cabling.

Wiring

Detailed wiring diagrams ensure safe and effective connections between components.

Current Prototype

Our prototype shows promising results in maneuverability and depth capability, with ongoing improvements.
